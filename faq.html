FAQ

Q: What is quantum mechanics?

A: Overall, quantum mechanics is a theory that describes the physics behind microscopic and nanoscale objects. This includes atoms, electrons, photons, phonons and any other type of quasiparticles. How these elements exist and interact with their environments describes the basis for quantum mechanics.

Q: Is only quantum mechanics taught on Schrödinger's Classroom?

A: Yes. The lessons provided on Schrödinger's Classroom are strictly related to quantum mechanics. However, because quantum mechanics is a specified subdivision of the overall subject of physics, many of its concepts relate back to and require the understanding of general and classical physics.

Q: How are your lessons organized?

A: Lessons on Schrödinger's Classroom are taught similar to a PowerPoint presentation, with linear organization going from slide to slide.
