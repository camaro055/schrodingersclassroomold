Introduction to Quantum Mechanics

Quantum mechanics is physics on the microscopic scale of atoms and electrons. At this small scale, the equaitons of Classical Mecahnics, which is used to describe how objects move at everday sizes and speeds, begins to break down and fall apart. For classical equations objects must exist in a specific place at a specific time, however, at the quantum level those condition become hazy. Within Quantum Mechanics objects exhibit a wavelike behaviour. Too understand this concept we can think of light as an example. Light can act like a wave, as well as, act like a particle in the form of photons. Due to these behaviours objects in Quantum Mechanics are then based off a probability of the object being at point A, another probability of it being at point B, and etc. Particles that form matter, such as electrons and atoms, have these same wavelike and particle behaviours. This exciting discovery of Wave-Particle Duality is the foundation of Quantum Mechanics.

The realizaton of the wavelike behaviour of particles sparked new discoveries that formed Qauntum Theory, which consists of; Schrödigners wave equation, the quantisation of light (Photons), the quantisation of matter, Young's double slit experiment, black body radiation, and many more mind boggiling topics. Throughout these lessons we hope to introduce you to these topics and give you more of an understand of want Quantum Mechanics is and some insite into the mysteries of our universe.
